Poster Tips

The purpose of a poster is to highlight the key questions and results of your academic activities and to present them in a visually attractive manner. Posters may describe interesting class projects, undergraduate or graduate research, works-in-progress, unique outcomes from a study abroad experience, etc.

  • Posters can be 24"x36" or 36"x48". We will provide three-fold cardboard poster holders for you to present your poster. Horizontal posters will fit best on the cardboard holders.
  • Poster examples (includes discussions of pluses and minuses, with explanations)
  • Creating Effective Poster Presentations (includes links to example posters)
  • ACM Crossroads Advice on Creating a Poster


The top poster will be recognized during the awards ceremony at Saturday's lunch. The winner must be present to receive the award. In the event of the awardee being absent, the second-place awardee will receive the award, and so on.

Lightning Tips

Lightning Talks are five minute mini-presentations that can range from a class project, research, inside information on how to survive a computing major, programming tips, ideas on how to create a successful women in computing group, etc.

  • Advice on How to Give a Good Lightning Talk by Mark Fowler
  • How to Conquer Public Speaking Fear (SlideShare) by Morton C. Orman, M.D.

You are encouraged to use one of the two templates below as you prepare your lightning talk. Each template has the transition timing already set up wherein slides automatically change every 15 or 30 seconds. You may modify the slide backgrounds and content as appropriate for your talk and should change the footers to identify your name, school, and title, but do NOT change the timing of the transitions or add/remove slides.

  • 10 slides that change ever 30 seconds
  • 20 slides that change every 15 seconds
